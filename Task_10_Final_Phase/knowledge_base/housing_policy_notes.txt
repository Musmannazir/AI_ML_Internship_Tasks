Housing policy notes

Affordable housing programs are designed to reduce cost burden for low- and moderate-income households.
Common policy tools include tax credits, inclusionary zoning, and rent assistance vouchers.
A recurring challenge is the mismatch between job growth in urban centers and available housing supply.
When supply growth is constrained, rents and sale prices typically rise faster than wages.

For practical guidance in city planning:
- Track housing starts and permit approvals quarterly.
- Compare rent-to-income ratios by district.
- Monitor vacancy rates for early signs of market stress.
